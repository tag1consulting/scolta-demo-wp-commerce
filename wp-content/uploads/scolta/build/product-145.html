Ilvaite Crystal — Miniature

Science and beauty are rarely in conflict. Here they converge. Named for Ilva (ancient Elba), ilvaite occurs in contact metamorphic skarns. The black prismatic crystals from Serifos are among the finest in the world, associated with epidote and calcite in combinations that show mineralogy’s talent for chromatic contrast. The Greek island setting adds a mythological resonance. This specimen originates from Serifos, Cyclades, Greece, one of the world’s most significant localities for this type of material. Collectors and scientists have drawn from this region for generations, and for good reason: the combination of geological conditions here produces specimens of exceptional quality and clarity. Every specimen is unique. Photographs approximate the visual experience, but the real thing — its weight, its luster under a raking light, the way it catches the corner of your eye — can only be experienced directly.
