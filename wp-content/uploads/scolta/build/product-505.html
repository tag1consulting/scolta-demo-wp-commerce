Jet Carved Specimen — Gem Rough

Science and beauty are rarely in conflict. Here they converge. Whitby jet — compressed fossil wood from 180-million-year-old monkey puzzle trees — became famous during Queen Victoria’s mourning period. Victorian jewelers carved it into elaborate pieces; Whitby’s jet workshops were legendary. The deep black, lightweight warmth, and workability of jet make it unique among organic gems. A carved piece is Victorian history in your hand. This specimen originates from Whitby, North Yorkshire, England, one of the world’s most significant localities for this type of material. Collectors and scientists have drawn from this region for generations, and for good reason: the combination of geological conditions here produces specimens of exceptional quality and clarity. Every specimen is unique. Photographs approximate the visual experience, but the real thing — its weight, its luster under a raking light, the way it catches the corner of your eye — can only be experienced directly.
