Labradorite Freeform — Specimen Grade

Consider what had to happen for this specimen to exist. Labradorescence — that ghostly play of blue, green, and gold — occurs when light diffracts between ultra-thin feldspar layers deposited during slow magmatic cooling. Each specimen is a prism masquerading as stone. Turn it in sunlight and it remembers something ancient. This specimen originates from Labrador, Canada, one of the world’s most significant localities for this type of material. Collectors and scientists have drawn from this region for generations, and for good reason: the combination of geological conditions here produces specimens of exceptional quality and clarity. Every specimen is unique. Photographs approximate the visual experience, but the real thing — its weight, its luster under a raking light, the way it catches the corner of your eye — can only be experienced directly.
