Emerald Crystal in Matrix — Cabinet Specimen

Science and beauty are rarely in conflict. Here they converge. Muzo Colombian emeralds are a geological anomaly — formed not in pegmatites like most beryls, but in black shales rich in carbon, chromium, and vanadium. The resulting color is the purest green in the mineral kingdom. Owning a Muzo emerald crystal in its original calcite matrix is owning a piece of the world’s most romanticized mine. This specimen originates from Muzo Mine, Boyacá, Colombia, one of the world’s most significant localities for this type of material. Collectors and scientists have drawn from this region for generations, and for good reason: the combination of geological conditions here produces specimens of exceptional quality and clarity. Every specimen is unique. Photographs approximate the visual experience, but the real thing — its weight, its luster under a raking light, the way it catches the corner of your eye — can only be experienced directly.
