Rough Emerald Crystal — Gem Grade — Gem Rough

The rarest things on Earth were formed by the most ordinary processes, repeated for eons. The Coscuez mine, one of three great Colombian emerald mines alongside Muzo and Chivor, produces a distinctive bright green. Colombian emeralds form in black shale and calcite veins — a hydrothermal system unique on Earth. A gem-grade rough crystal still in matrix represents the stone before the cutter’s art intervenes: the raw material of history’s most coveted colored gem. This specimen originates from Coscuez Mine, Boyacá, Colombia, one of the world’s most significant localities for this type of material. Collectors and scientists have drawn from this region for generations, and for good reason: the combination of geological conditions here produces specimens of exceptional quality and clarity. Every specimen is unique. Photographs approximate the visual experience, but the real thing — its weight, its luster under a raking light, the way it catches the corner of your eye — can only be experienced directly.
