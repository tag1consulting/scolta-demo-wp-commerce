Vanadinite on Barite — Miniature

The rarest things on Earth were formed by the most ordinary processes, repeated for eons. Vanadinite’s intense red-orange hexagonal prisms on white barite matrix are one of mineralogy’s most photogenic combinations. This Moroccan locality is the world standard for the species. The color comes from vanadium — the same element used to strengthen steel — here precipitated from oxidizing lead ore deposits. This specimen originates from Mibladen, Khénifra, Morocco, one of the world’s most significant localities for this type of material. Collectors and scientists have drawn from this region for generations, and for good reason: the combination of geological conditions here produces specimens of exceptional quality and clarity. Every specimen is unique. Photographs approximate the visual experience, but the real thing — its weight, its luster under a raking light, the way it catches the corner of your eye — can only be experienced directly.
